// about

Where Biology
Meets Computation

I am an Assistant Professor at CSVTU Bhilai, working at the intersection of computational biology, bioinformatics, and machine learning. My research spans theoretical modelling and experimental validation — with a core focus on how genetic mutations shape the fitness of living organisms.

From mapping the fitness landscapes of E. coli at IIT Bombay to studying metabolic networks at Virginia Tech and collaborating on precision oncology models at the Medical College of Wisconsin, my work bridges the molecular and the mathematical.

Beyond the bench, I lead MOOC courses on SWAYAM, drive CSVTU's innovation ecosystem as IIC President, and mentor the next generation of computational biologists.

The Research Journey

From engineering fundamentals to the frontier of computational biology — across four cities and three countries of collaboration.

Assistant Professor · Department of Biomedical Engineering & Bioinformatics
Leading research in ML, bioinformatics, and computational biology. HoD in charge, IIC President, and coordinator of MOOC courses on SWAYAM (Microbial Technology, Computational Biology, Genetic Engineering). April 2020 → December 2020 Post-Doc
Virginia Tech, Blacksburg
Post-Doctoral Scholar · Department of Biological Sciences · Advisor: Prof. Jeremy Draghi
Metabolic and fitness landscape construction of Methylobacterium extorquens. Bridging experimental evolution with computational landscape theory in a highly collaborative environment.

Thesis: "Theoretical and experimental studies in microbial evolution." Eight years of investigation into how mutations shape organismal fitness — modelling genetic regulatory networks, designing evolutionary experiments, and publishing foundational work in systems biology.
