About

Research Focus & Background

Dr. Brajesh is an Assistant Professor at CSVTU Bhilai, working across computational biology, bioinformatics, and machine learning. His research spans theoretical modelling and experimental validation, centred on how genetic mutations shape the fitness of living organisms.

His doctoral and postdoctoral work mapped the fitness landscapes of E. coli at IIT Bombay and the metabolic networks of Methylobacterium extorquens at Virginia Tech. He continues to collaborate with the Medical College of Wisconsin on precision-oncology and cardiotoxicity models.

At CSVTU, he leads MOOC courses on the SWAYAM platform, serves as President of the Institute Innovation Cell, and has secured over ₹15 crore in national grants for the university's innovation infrastructure.

Research interests — Machine Learning · Microbial Evolution · Bioinformatics · Fitness Landscapes · Computational Biology · Drug Design · Cell Physiology · Systems Biology

Experimental Research
ELISA assays, bacterial gene knockouts, evolutionary experiments, and murine handling.
Theoretical Modelling
